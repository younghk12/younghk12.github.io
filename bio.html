Bio

I am searching for roles in user experience (UX) / user interface (UI) / digital product design, involving elements of information architecture and brand identity design. My ideal design role would be breaking down complexity, whether literally—organizing and clarifying large amounts of information in a design layout—or more abstractly—using visual storytelling to communicate complicated ideas (such as the gravity of an unfamiliar new technology). I want to collaborate closely with software developers (and others from all types of disciplines) to make thoughtful digital products.

My design background is slightly unorthodox, however planning my own major allowed me to get the most out of my liberal arts education. Art and art history gave me a conceptual foundation for design with the visual principals, the skills to do in-depth visual analyses, as well as an overall understanding of visual and idealistic trends. Expanding outside of the theory, I sought out practical experience with different softwares from design and cs classes; computer science makes me suited for roles in software and makes me an effective collaborator with software developers. Admittedly, design software was the weakest link in my education, but I have since augmented with several online resources. Through the liberal arts I explored a wide range of academics, drawing connections between assumed distinct subjects, discovering new ways in which they inform one another, and gaining the soft skills for collaboration across these disciplines.
