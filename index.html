Bio

is a designer and new media artist based in Portland, Oregon. He recently graduated from Whitman College majoring in Theory and Practice in Digital Design. The individually planned major combined new media art, computer science, and art history with a focus on modern art and architecture. The experimental major served as an unofficial model for the Human Centered Design major introduced the next year. Henry realized he was interested in design in highschool after taking a summer course at Sci-Arc. He uses processing, figma, and various coding languages to create interactive interfaces, reflecting his Brutalist inspired style. He is familiar with python, C++, java, and html/javascript. Henry hopes to explore a career in User Experience Design or Architecture.

Culture

A developer with an artistic sense, a designer with functional aspirations

In a quickly evolving world with increasing demands and emerging technologies, specialization is leading to narrow thinking, reduced flexibility, issues collaborating, and ultimately, limited chances for creative cross pollination. A more multifaceted approach can bridge the gaps and creates a more impactful, cohesive product.

Design is more than making beautiful screens

With our tools doing more and more for us, making pretty things becomes easier and easier. Creating a coherent yet flexible design system is difficult. Strong, informed conceptual thinking can help sift through a myriad of choices.
